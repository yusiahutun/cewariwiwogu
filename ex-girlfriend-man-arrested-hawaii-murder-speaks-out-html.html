Ex-girlfriend of man arrested for Hawaii murder speaks out

April 2024 Â· 5 minute read

The man arrested and accused of beating a Hawaiian house cleaner to death has a history of domestic abuse, court documents reveal, as donors raise more than $50,000 for the victim's eight-year-old daughter.

Stephen Brown, 23, and his girlfriend Hailey Kai Dandurand, 20, were taken into custody late Thursday after Telma Boinville was found beaten to death with a baseball bat inside a Honolulu holiday home. 

The couple were tracked down by police after they allegedly sped off in the dead woman's vehicle. 

And court documents acquired by KITV show that in June Brown was arrested after his former girlfriend claimed he abused her.

The Department of Public Safety told the network that Brown posted bail from OCCC in July while awaiting trial. 

'The last time I talk to him, he told me that he didn't have a job and he was very suicidal and that he was planning to do death by cop, something stupid like pretending you have a gun so the cop will shoot you, so I think this is his plan so he gets beaten to death in prison,'  the woman told KITV. 

Boinville was murdered Thursday afternoon in the rental home she was working in, according to deputy police chief John McCarthy.  It was only when the renters arrived that her body was found in one of the rooms.

The victim's eight-year-old daughter, Makana, was found uninjured but tied up in a separate room with a bag placed over her head.

And on Friday night another Hawaii local, Cali Goldberg, started a GoFundMe to raise money for Makana.

Donations immediately came pouring in, and in just one day after the site was set live it had already raised more than $51,000. 

Brown and Dandurand were hauled into police cruisers on Friday and driven away. And the more than 60 onlookers who came to watch the action shouted angrily at the accused killers.

Kevin Emery, the victim's husband, had confirmed to the Hawaii News Now he was banned from entering the home because the bloody scene was so horrific. 

'They tied up my daughter and they beat my wife to death with a baseball bat,' he said at the time. 'They won't even let me in the house because it's so bad.'
