Gingerbread Truck Recipe - Country Gingerbread House Ideas

February 2024 Â· 4 minute read

Here's what you'll need:

Gingerbread truck templates

Gingerbread Cookie Dough

Tree-shaped cookie cutters

Royal Icing

Sturdy base of white painted board

Cardboard or 1/4-inch-thick foam core

Piping bags and small round tips

Jars or cans

Round butter cookies, gray nonpareils, pretzel rods, green food coloring, white nonpareils, red food coloring, shortbread dog cookies, and confectioners' sugar for decorating.

Instructions:

1. Print templates and cut out.

2. Bake Gingerbread Cookie Dough as instructed. Use any leftover non-dyed dough to roll, cut, and bake trees.

3. Prepare Royal Icing as instructed.*

4. Reserve half of the icing. Spread remaining icing on white board, leaving a smooth path to resemble a road. Let dry.

5. Using the template, cut 5 TRUCK BED pieces 1 inch smaller all the way around out of cardboard or foam core. Stack, and tape together. This will support the truck.

6. Fill a piping bag halfway with icing. Snip a 1/8-inch opening in the end.

7. Pipe icing along edges of FRONT CAB, BACK CAB, RIGHT DOOR, and LEFT DOOR. Place pieces together, propping with cans or jars until set.

8. Pipe icing along edges of ENGINE LEFT, ENGINE RIGHT, and FRONT GRILL. Place pieces together following the contours of the TRUCK BED piece. Place HOOD on top. Let dry until set.

9. Place truck bed on cardboard support. Place cab and engine boxes on TRUCK BED. Pipe along edges to adhere.

10. Pipe icing around bottom half of butter cookie and place on inside of FRONT CAB to resemble a steering wheel.

11. Pipe icing along top edges of cab and top with ROOF, with curved edge toward front.

12. Pipe icing on bottom and side edges of TRUCK BED SIDES. Attach to back of truck bed. Let dry until set.

13. Pipe icing on inside bottom of WOOD PANEL pieces. Place on outside of TRUCK BED SIDES. Pipe icing on top edges of truck cab and top with ROOF, curved side in front of truck. Let dry until set.

14. Pipe spokes on FRONT WHEELS and BACK WHEELS. Place a small dot of icing in center of wheels; top with gray nonpareils. Pipe icing on back of wheels and attach to truck.

15. Pipe icing on back of LEFT FENDER and RIGHT FENDER. Place over front wheels. Pipe icing on back of headlights; place on top sides of grill. Let dry.

16. Pipe lines down center of truck grill; attach gray nonpareils. Pipe decorative lines along top and sides of truck, if desired.

17. Place truck (with support) on prepared base.

18. Break a few pretzel rods in half. Working one section at a time, use a dollup of icing to attach broken pretzels to full-length pretzels to resemble a fence. Let dry until set. Pipe a large dollop of icing where "fence posts" will be set. Prop with jars or cans. Let dry and set.

19. Tint about 1 cup reserved icing to desired shade of green. Transfer to a small piping bag and snip a 1/8-inch hole in the corner. Pipe or frost on trees; decorate with white nonpareils. Place trees in or around truck, as desired. If placing on base around truck, set in a dollop of white icing and prop with jars or cans. Let dry until set.

20. Tint about 2 tablespoons reserved icing to desired shade of red. Transfer to a small piping bag and snip a 1/8-inch hole in the corner. Pipe around neck of Scottie dog to resemble a collar. Pipe a small dot of white icing on eye; top with gray nonpareil.

21. Lightly dust truck and fence with confectioners' sugar to resemble freshly fallen snow.

*Traditionalists use edible Royal Icing to assemble their creations, but it can take awhile for the icing to dry. If you are not planning on eating the gingerbread and want to assemble quickly, consider using hot-glue to secure the pieces.
