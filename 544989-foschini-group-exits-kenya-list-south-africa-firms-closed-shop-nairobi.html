Foschini Group Exits Kenya: List of Other South Africa Firms That Closed Shop In Nairobi

May 2024 · 4 minute read
  • Foschini Group Kenya Limited announced its exit from the Kenyan market effective April 2024
  • The South Africa-based fashion brand appointed two joint liquidators in Kenya to clear claims from creditors in the country
  • The company joined a list of other South African multinational companies that exited the country, including Builders Warehouse

TUKO.co.ke journalist Wycliffe Musalia brings over five years of experience in financial, business, and technology reporting, offering deep insights into Kenyan and global economic trends.

South African-based company Foschini Group has wound up its operations in Nairobi, Kenya.

The fashion brand, which deals in clothing, jewellery accessories, sporting and outdoor equipment, as well as homeware, said the move was agreed during a special general meeting on April 5, 2024.

Foschini Group Kenya appointed Messrs George Weru and Muniu Thoithi as the joint liquidators of the business.

Why companies are exiting Kenya

It directed all creditors to submit their claims to the liquidators on or before April 15, 2024.

“Creditors of the company are required on or before May 15 2024 to send full particulars of all the claims they may have against the company to the undersigned, the Joint Liquidators of the Company,” read Foschini Group Kenya notice in part, as quoted by Capital Business.

Foschini Group Kenya joins a list of other South Africa-based multinational companies that exited Kenya.

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau explained that the changing taxation policy in the country is hurting the operating environment and pushing away investors.

"High taxes will end up hurting the tax base. The super-rich people find comfort in a country with a stable taxation policy, a stable currency, and a growing economy. The current progress of the current administration is changing tax policy rapidly, mismanaging the currency, and weakening economic growth," said Kamau.

List of South African firms that exited Kenya

1. Foschini Group

The company set its base in Kenya in 2016 and has been operating with stores at the Junction Mall and the Nairobi Village Market.

It boasts a network of more than 2,100 stores located across Africa.

2. Builders Warehouse

Builders retail store announced its exit from the Kenyan market, bowing to poor economic pressure.

The chain store that was located at the Waterfront Karen, Nairobi made the announcement following the exit of other brands.

The company set its base in Kenya in August 2020 and operated for less than three years.

3. The Game Stores

South African retailer The Game Stores exited the Kenyan market, closing its final shops in December 2022.

The firm cited increased losses after rival supermarkets snubbed a deal to buy some of its remaining stores.

4. Shoprite

The South African retailer Shoprite joined the Kenyan market in 2018.

Is there any South African firm in Kenya?

Apart from the mass exits, other South Africa firms still consider Kenya as an economic hub for the East Africa region.

In January 2024, global retail shop Panda Mart opened its first outlet at Garden City Mall in Kenya's capital, Nairobi.

The South African chain store, operating in more than 12 African countries, chose Kenya as an entry into the East African market.

Panda Mart director revealed that the company invested over KSh 1.1 billion into the Kenyan venture.
