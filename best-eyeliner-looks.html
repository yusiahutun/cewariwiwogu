24 Cool Eyeliner Looks for 2023

September 2024 · 4 minute read
1

Cool Blue

Dark blue speaks volumes while still feeling somewhat neutral. Try a wing—or if you’ve mastered that, add a thin line along your crease for a bold look like Jodie Turner-Smith's.

2

Coral Crush

You don't need to create a full line to pack a punch. Stick to colors that stand out like Khloé Kardashian's coral and draw a small line on your crease stopping at your pupil. For a softer effect, buff the line out with a similar-colored shadow.

3

Cult Classic

When in doubt, go for a simple black wing like Sydney Sweeney's. Use a felt-tip liner so you can easily adjust the thickness based on how much pressure you apply.

4

Foil Finish

5

Rainbow Eyes

Why pick one color when you can showcase an array of hues? Try a rainbow of colors, shown here, or opt for different shades of a single color. Top the liner with a matching shadow to add vibrancy and staying power.

6

Back to Mod

JC Olivera//Getty Images

Channel the '60s with this graphic winged liner seen on Olivia Rodrigo that's easier than it looks. The trick to a perfect point: Use a precession liner and pointed Q-tip for (inevitable) cleanup.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

Frosted Tips

Frazer Harrison//Getty Images

Channel the '90s with an icy blue eyeliner, which winks to the decade without going full frosted blue eye shadow. To make the blue pop, apply a black liner in your upper waterline and a soft pink shadow on your lid.

8

Smoke Show

Follow Emily Ratajkowski's lead and go for the modern smoky eye: Blend out a liner into a wing shape on your upper and lower lash lines for a dramatic effect.

9

Straight to the Point

This runway eyeliner look from the fall/winter 2022 Christian Siriano show by Charlotte Tilbury is giving major Matrix vibes. Makeup artist Sofia Tilbury added this cornflower blue hue (a mix of Eye Colour Magic Liner Duo in Super Blue and concealer) on the lid right below the crease and finished with a swipe of black eyeliner for the perfect contrast.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

Blinged Out

We said it once, we'll say it again: Euphoria eyes are all the rage now. A simple way to dive into the trend is to add a few gemstones to the outer corner of your eye to complement a simple black wing.

11

Neon Lights

Emma Chamberlain's Met Gala 2021 liner was arguably the main attraction of her look that night. Adding a pop of neon to the center of the lash line and the inner corner lightens up a black smoky eye.

12

Quick Flick

Searching for an everyday eye-makeup look? Look no further. This soft baby wing can be done in a few seconds. The key is not to be too precious about it: Quickly flick your pencil upward, following your bottom lash line, for an effortless wing.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

13

Emerald Eyes

Emerald green makes eyes pop and is the perfect transition shade for summer to fall. This metallic halo eye version is too pretty not to re-create.

14

Paint the Town

Accentuate your eye shape with neon liner in your inner corner. The bright color brings the focus to your eyes and makes you look more awake.

15

Grunge Girl

Zoë Kravitz is the ultimate cool girl, and this look embodies that. To re-create, smudge a kohl pencil with a brush—or your finger for a more lived-in effect—and finish by lining your upper and bottom waterlines. To prevent your look from running, add black shadow on top.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

16

Waterline Comeback

Jon Kopaloff//Getty Images

Bottom liner is making a comeback (we can thank tastemakers like Rihanna and Lupita Nyong'o), but in a colorful way. Just one swipe of bright line can make an impact.

17

Reverse Liner

Reverse eyeliner isn't just for Coachella. To balance the smudged, smoky bottom, keep the top lash line clean with just a swipe of mascara.

18

Shimmer Shades

Bold color mixed with sparkle feels fresh. Apply a glitter shadow across your entire lid up to your brow bone and a swipe of colored liner. Try the same hue as the sparkle shade for a monochromatic look.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

19

Shadow Liner

If you prefer moodier looks, buff out your eyeliner so it appears softer than a harsh line. Try a gel liner, which has more time to work with than liquid or kohl textures, celebrity makeup artist Jamie Greenberg says.

20

In Between

An easy way to incorporate color into an eye look? Sandwich bright color between two black swipes of eyeliner. Buff out the edges with a beige shadow for an effortless look.
