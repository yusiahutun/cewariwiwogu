Anastacia reveals her mastectomy scars as she poses naked for stunning new shoot

July 2024 · 5 minute read

She told fans she entered Strictly Come Dancing this year to reclaim her femininity.

And while Anastacia, 48, is out of the BBC competition she's still on her mission, with her next move being a naked photoshoot to shot her mastectomy scars for the first time.

The US singer poses for the new issue of Fault Magazine, showing off her figure and her scars in a series of stunning images.

Brave: Anastacia has posed naked for a new shoot with Fault Magazine to show her mastectomy scars for the first ime

The star, who donated her Strictly fee to breast cancer research, has been open about her battle with cancer, making the decision to have a double mastectomy after two battles with breast cancer in 2003 and then ten years later in 2013.

As part of reconstructive surgery she had the latissimus dorsi muscles removed from her back and attached to her front to rebuild her chest, leaving scars on both her chest and back.

But until now she's never revealed her scars, deciding finally to strip off to reveal her body on her own terms. 

'I’m nervous to let people see them, I really am, but I want to be able to go on the beach and not have the first photographs taken of my body to be ones that I didn’t give,' she explains to Fault.

Revealing all: The star had a double mastectomy three years ago after two battles with breast cancer but until now she's never revealed her scars

'I want to be free and knowing that this photo-shoot will be out there and available, I can be on the beach and not worry about what people think.'

'Paparazzi can take a photo of me breathing in and call it a gut or say all the things they want, that doesn’t scare me but my scars are a part of me and I want to be the one to reveal them. If paparazzi photographed them, I’d feel like I was being shamed but being able to release them this way, I feel extremely empowered.'

Anastacia adds that it's taken her a long time to come to terms with her scars, which are longer than some mastectomy scars because surgeons had to work around her tattoos, but her stint on Strictly has helped build her confidence and stop 'resenting them.'   

Confidence: Anastacia adds that it's taken her time to come to terms with her scars, which are longer than some mastectomy scars because surgeons had to work around her tattoos

'Every little thing that I’m doing, including Strictly Come Dancing was me trying to reclaim a little piece of my femininity that cancer stole from me. When you have as many surgeries as I’ve had, you lose so many female sensations that you’ll never get back,' she candidly admits.

'Nipple sensation is something that cancer robbed from my anatomy and I’m used to it now but there is that little part of me that will always be lost. Doing Strictly and doing this shoot I feel like I’m finally becoming the woman that I was again.'

Anastacia is working with Cancer Research to stress the importance of mammograms, while she's also establishing a chat forum for women to talk and discuss cancer with people who have actually been through it all.

Life changing: The US singer says her time on Strictly Come Dancing this year has helped build her confidence and stop 'resenting' her scars

The interview also sees the star open up on the injury she picked up on the Strictly dance floor, which led to the controversial decision to allow her to skip the dance off. 

'I just thought it was a sore muscle and I just waited for it to pass but it got progressively worse and then I felt a lump which I knew couldn’t be cancer because I didn’t have any breast tissue left,' she explains. 

A trip to the doctor just hours before the live Saturday night show confirmed she had inflamed scar tissue but she and partner Brendan Cole decided to continue with their performance, which left her in incredible pain at the end.  

Pals: The singer was parterned with Brendan Cole during her time on Strictly

'Then I was told there was a dance off. I knew I was physically unable to perform, and I was terrified about what damage I had already done,' she says after finding herself in the bottom two alongside Melvin Odoom, only to be told that instead the public vote would decide who would be going home - leading to Melvin's exit.

'I began to cry and said "sorry I’ll get my things and go and thanks for the opportunity" and I was ready to leave …I didn't know they had contingencies for occurrences like that.’ 

It's set to be an exciting few months for the singer as she prepares to tour the UK early next summer with her Ultimate Collection Tour.
