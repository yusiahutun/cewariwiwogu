Affordable Clothing Stores to Shop 2024

March 2024 · 9 minute read

All products and services featured are independently chosen by editors. However, StyleCaster may receive a commission on orders placed through its retail links, and the retailer may receive certain auditable data for accounting purposes.

Whenever we’re experiencing a fashion emergency, Zara often comes to the rescue. In need of a fun wedding guest dress? Zara. Searching for something cute to wear on vacation? Zara, yet again. But as much as we love shopping at this store, we don’t mind switching things up from time to time. After all, there are a handful of other brands like Zara that deserve our attention, too, and whether you’re in the market for sleek wardrobe staples, statement-making pieces, or playful accessories, you don’t want to miss out on some of these gems.

When compiling our list of brands like Zara, we did consider some important criteria. These stores needed to offer similar price points, and while they might be fast fashion, the clothes had to look expensive and feel well-made. Some of our favorite Zara buys have been designer dupes, so when it comes to brands that are comparable to Zara, they should offer more of the same.

Check out a few of our other favorite places to shop, ahead, which range from retailers you might know and already love to rising stars worth adding to your list.

  • Mango

    Mango, like Zara, is great at keeping up with current trends and selling luxe-looking yet budget-friendly items. Aside from all the dreamy clothing and accessories this store has to offer — which, yes, comes in women’s, men’s, teens’, and kids’ sizing — the store also has a wide selection of home goods worth perusing.

    Just like Zara, Mango is a great place to shop for event outfits along with everyday essentials. Unlike Zara, however, the store is a bit more inclusive with its sizing, and dresses such as this textured floral design are available in sizes 0 through 20.

    While Mango does sell a few splurge-worthy pieces (some of its more elaborate designs might set you back a few hundred dollars), a lot of its stuff is reasonably priced. For example, if you’re in the market for a quality polo, you’ll be able to score one for less than $40.

    The older we get, the more we realize that keeping your home looking cute isn’t always easy. Thankfully, Mango sells cushion covers, sheet sets, and other fun decor that can quickly transform any old house into a happier, cooler, and cozier place.

  • NA-KD

    If your style is best described as somewhat simple with a fashion-forward twist, NA-KD is bound to become one of your new favorite spots. A quick scroll will lead you to a feel-good mix of accordion-pleated sets, off-the-shoulder tops, and knit dresses with lace-up details. In other words, this brand is all about cool meets comfort — which, if you ask us, is a winning combination.

    This knitted maxi dress is so soft that it’ll almost feel like you’re pulling on pajamas before heading out the door. However, that lace-up back detail turns this staple into something sexy enough for a winter date night.

    Fashion lovers know that a faux leather jacket is the ultimate must-have, especially during transitional months. This oversized NA-KD bomber is a modern update to a timeless trend, and — the ultimate bonus! — is roomy enough to layer a bulky sweater or two underneath.

    There’s a time and place for a plain white tee, but if your go-to pair of jeans could use a bit more spice, NA-KD sells basics that are, well, not so basic. While this green top might feel as comfy as your favorite crewneck, it’ll add interest to your look thanks to its unique twisted design and surprise hood.

  • Aritzia

    If there’s one thing to know about Aritzia, it’s that this brand sells clothes you both want and need. Its famously warm Super Puff™ coat is beloved by stars such as Emily Ratajkowski, and tons of influencers have been spotted rocking the store’s famous Tna leggings. Beyond that, Artizia’s has quite a collection of quality staples, such as wear-everywhere trousers and T-shirts you can’t see through.

    When it comes to versatile bottoms, look no further than The Effortless Pant™. We love the easy, loose fit and the fact you’re able to find your perfect length by choosing between short, regular, or tall sizes.

    We’re not exaggerating when we say that this coat is comparable to a pricy Canada Goose parka. It’s filled with goose down and engineered to deliver warmth up to -4°F. Plus, there are tons of ways to make it all your own, since Aritizia has a whole section of its retail site dedicated to customization.

    Whether you’re running on the treadmill or lounging on the couch, this stretchy jumpsuit is the perfect one-and-done look. Take it out of the house by copying style star Sydney Sweeney, who paired the piece with long white socks, sporty sneakers, and a puffer coat.

  • ASOS

    ASOS sells a bunch of different brands, but the site’s own label is how you’ll get the most bang for your buck. Just like Zara, it keeps up with what the top celebrities and influencers are wearing and tends to be ahead of the game when it comes to recreating runway-approved trends.

    Part of what makes ASOS stand out among the pack is its inclusive sizing. Its Curve line makes it easier to shop cool clothes without sacrificing your love of style, and whether you’re getting dressed up for date night or simply want to rock a button-down vest with baggy trousers, you’re bound to find what you’re searching for here.

    While we mainly know Zara for its clothing, the store also sells beauty products like makeup and fragrances, and ASOS does the same. The one difference is that ASOS’ Face + Body section is packed with well-known labels such as e.l.f. and MAC, so if Ulta is sold out of your favorites, you may be able to find them in this mix.

    If splurging on accessories isn’t for you, you’ll be thrilled as you browse ASOS’ little extras. Aside from listing luxe-looking earrings for less than $10, these sleek sunglasses are just $15.

  • H&M

    Another trusty mall brand, H&M is probably the place you hit up either before or after a trip to Zara. The price points are similar, the clothes are comparable, and within minutes, you’ll likely find your cart filled with everything you want and need, whether that’s gifts for babies in your life, sharp-looking menswear, or home decor.

    If your T-shirt collection could use an update, you’ll find some of the best deals on H&M’s site. The brand sells multi-packs of items like tees and tanks, along with sets of undergarments, socks, and even pajama shorts.

    $10.5013.9925% Off

    Jeans trends are always changing, so if you’re itching to try a cut you don’t typically wear, check out this store’s denim offerings. You’ll be able to experiment with wide-leg pairs and tapered styles among others, and while these pants could pass as designer, most ring under $50. Even better is that some go up to size 20.

    $22.5029.9925% Off

    When we say H&M sells decor, we mean it sells the stuff you wish you had in your house. Fill your cart with a few of its statement-making pieces — including this side table, some candles, rugs, and throw blankets — and your apartment will look ready for an Architectural Digest tour.

  • Cider

    If you’ve ever spotted a trending aesthetic and wondered how you could incorporate it into your wardrobe without going broke, the answer is Cider. This brand is essentially Zara’s more youthful, quirkier sister and also has a smart business model: everything is made “on demand,” so the store doesn’t overproduce and is more conscious about waste.

    Do stores even sell bikinis in sets anymore? And if they do, are any of them under $100? Yes and yes! Cider’s swimwear collection is extensive and, shockingly, this versatile two-piece will cost you just $18.

    Shoe lovers won’t be disappointed when shopping on Cider. While the brand sells a ton of fun footwear, such as heart-shaped heels, you’ll also be able to find timeless classics like preppy, patent leather loafers.

    For those of us who have lusted after Jenna Rink’s dress from 13 Going On 30, here’s a little treat: Cider sells a near-identical version for just $20. Even if you’re not willing to rock it on an average night out or slip in on for a milestone birthday (which is definitely a good idea!), it’s a great one to have on hand for Halloween.

  • PrettyLittleThing

    If it’s good enough for Naomi Campbell, it’s good enough for us. PrettyLittleThing has almost every women’s fashion category covered — sexy staples, basics, workwear, etc. — and new items are regularly added, so you won’t miss out on whatever is trending. The brand has collaborated with multiple celebrities (and a handful of Love Island alums) and just like Zara, it’s a great store to shop when you’re hoping to give your wardrobe a boost but don’t want to shell out thousands.

    Sometimes, an oversized blazer is all you need to pull an outfit together. Since this brown houndstooth design is pretty neutral, you’ll likely reach for it time and time again, styling it with everything from casual jeans to slinky black dresses.

This draped mid-length creation — which is also available in plus sizes — is sexy and elegant, and since it’s somewhat simple, you’ll be able to amp it up with accessories of your choice.

  • COS

    COS is one store that’s constantly compared to Zara. While its pricing is a tad more expensive, this store makes the eye-catching, elevated basics you’ll love for years to come. If you’re a fan of Zara’s knits and button-downs or are constantly lusting after stuff from The Row, you’re bound to become a big fan of this retailer.

    No closet is complete without a denim jacket and this one comes with a cool-girl spin. But while the moto design makes the staple seem tougher, this piece will still come in handy during those transitional months and will no doubt pair well with sundresses, leather skirts, and linen shorts.

    While basic button-downs have a reputation for being boring, we’ve come to realize we need them in our closet. Sleek collared shirts are a great way to balance out elaborate bottoms, but if you’re tired of the classic everyday options, try this alternative, which includes puff sleeves and a minimalistic collar.

    Men’s accessories sections sometimes feel like they’re lacking, but not at COS. Within the store’s mix, we found this geometric chain, which will not only look great when layered with similar necklaces but would also make a great gift.
