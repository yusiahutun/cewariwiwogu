Scooby-Doo is Inspired By The Great Dane

When you type “What Kind of Dog is Scooby-Doo?” on Google, the search engine presents the Great Dane as the famous dog’s breed. Upon looking closely, you may see the resemblance between Scooby and the large dog breed. Scooby-Doo’s breed has been a subject of debate for a long time. Some people just don’t accept that you can own a dog just like Scooby-Doo from almost anywhere in the world. In truth, Great Danes do not speak in broken English or eat hamburgers with their hands. However, these canines offer the closest features you need to own a real-life Scooby-Doo.

Despite the endless debates, we already know the answer to “What Kind of Dog is Scooby-Doo?” According to the canine’s original animator, Iwao Takamoto, he created Scooby with a Great Dane in mind. In truth, he consulted a fellow staff member who breeds Great Dane dogs. Thanks to this inquiry, Takamoto came up with the goofy canine we came to know and love. However, it’s easy to understand why some people disagree with this answer. The original animator’s words don’t seem to be enough proof to some fans of the show.

Other Possible Scooby-Doo Dog Breeds

After answering “What kind of dog is Scooby-Doo?” we must delve into the mystery behind the Great Dane’s appearance. Experts from the American Kennel Club consider them as the largest dog breed in terms of height. This fact can easily be seen from the cartoon since Scooby appears taller than Shaggy and Fred when he stands on his two hind legs. When it comes to its origins, the Great Dane was the result of a crossbreed between English mastiffs and Irish wolfhounds. English mastiffs hail as the heaviest dogs among all large dog breeds. Because of their height and weight, these mastiffs received training for blood sports like assisting in the hunting of bears. Meanwhile, people trained Irish wolfhounds to chase and attack wolves. As one of the oldest dog breeds, Irish wolfhounds became war hounds and served to guard soldiers.

It may be hard to imagine how the Great Dane became the result of crossbreeding English mastiffs and Irish wolfhounds. However, we urge you to look at the similar details found in all three dog breeds. Great Danes got their shiny coats and size from English mastiffs. Meanwhile, the facial features and length of Irish wolfhounds depict a few similarities with Great Danes. The combination of the two breeds resulted in the fixed features of the Great Dane that we know of today.

When Did Scooby-Doo Come Out?

Scooby-Doo officially graced our TV screens in 1969. Before then, one of Hanna-Barbera’s Saturday morning cartoons featured a glimpse of the soon-to-be Scooby in “Mysteries Five.” In the episode, the animators designed a dog who loved to play the bongo and named him “Too Much.” However, the animated canine’s breed confused audiences due to their frequent changes. In some scenes, he looked like a Great Dane while in others he depicted a sheepdog. Eventually, Hanna Barbara released “Scooby-Doo, Where Are You!” which took the world by storm. It spawned several sequels and continues to deliver mysteries to old and young fans.

Initially, Hanna-Barbera came up with Scooby-Doo’s story in hopes of alleviating protests of several parent groups. Back then, adults expressed their outrage over the alleged violent influence of superhero-themed shows that aired during the weekends. After several changes, Hanna-Barbera’s team eventually came up with the premise we know and love. Despite those revisions, the creators never veered off from their concept of producing a cartoon about supernatural mysteries solved by a group of teenagers and their dogs. The first installment also featured the famous Scooby-Doo catchphrase “And I’d have gotten away with it too if it weren’t for those meddling kids!”

Meaning Behind the “Scooby-doo-be-doo-be-doo” Catchphrase

Fred Silverman served as one of the main producers who made the release of the Scooby-Doo franchise. Thanks to his efforts, Hanna-Barbera found a way to release the mystery-solving antics of Scooby-Doo and the gang. Aside from that, Silverman pitched the “Scooby-doo-be-doo-be-doo” catchphrase we know and love. He got the idea from Frank Sinatra’s “Strangers in the Night.” However, when you listen to the song, you might notice the difference between Sinatra’s tune to Scooby’s high-pitched “doo-bee-doo-bee-doo. In truth, Silverman suggested the catchphrase but he didn’t say anything about the tone. Plus, it doesn’t depict any true meaning aside from being a catchy line for Scooby-Doo. You may think of it as an adlib that caught the attention of fans and eventually became a catchphrase. Most signature lines from iconic shows start that way.

What is Velma’s Sexuality?

In the storyline of “Scooby-Doo! Mystery Incorporated,” we were all shocked to find out about Shaggy and Velma’s romantic relationship. However, their budding romance didn’t last as long as Fred and Daphne’s long-term relationship. You probably cringed when Scooby-Doo caught them sneaking a makeout session in the garden. In truth, the writers of the show intended for the couple to depict something cringe-worthy than sweet and romantic. We all sighed in relief when Shaggy and Velma finally broke up and remained friends in the show’s 10th episode. Because of this display, we all assumed that Velma identifies as a heterosexual. However, the true intentions of the show’s writers revealed that they had something else in mind.

Aside from the sudden romance between Shaggy and Velma, “Scooby-Doo! Mystery Incorporated” showcased Velma’s rivalry with Marcie, a fellow science genius. Some fans felt the romantic tension between the two characters despite seeing Velma’s relationship with Shaggy. In truth, some of us hoped that a budding romance blossomed between Velma and Marcie instead. Unbeknownst to many, the show’s producers initially leaned to depicting Velma as a lesbian. Producer Tony Cervone claimed that they intended the romantic tension between Velma and Marcie while the two characters bump heads. The producers eventually agreed to identify Velma as bisexual in hopes of clearing out any misunderstandings between the character’s romance with Shaggy.
