Kommos is an Ancient Minoan Site

Kommos is an ancient Minoan site located on the southern coast of Crete in Greece. It was a thriving settlement during the Bronze Age and played a significant role in trade and commerce in the Mediterranean.

It Dates Back to the Late Minoan Period

Kommos was inhabited during the Late Minoan period, specifically around 1800-1100 BCE. It was a prosperous and cosmopolitan center, known for its trading activities and strategic location.

It Was a Major Port City

Kommos was a major port city, with a well-developed harbor that facilitated maritime trade between Crete and other Mediterranean civilizations. Its strategic location contributed to its economic and cultural significance.

It Features Impressive Archaeological Discoveries

Excavations at Kommos have unearthed a wealth of archaeological treasures, including ancient buildings, pottery, Minoan frescoes, and even evidence of a Minoan shipwreck. These discoveries provide valuable insights into the ancient Minoan civilization.

Kommos Was a Center for Trade and Exchange

As a major port city, Kommos served as a hub for trade and exchange between the Minoans and other civilizations of the time, such as Egypt, the Near East, and the Mycenaean Greeks. It played a vital role in the regional economy.

It Has a Complex Architectural Layout

The architectural layout of Kommos is characterized by a network of streets, buildings, and courtyards. The site showcases the Minoan architectural style, with well-preserved remnants of houses, workshops, and public spaces.

Kommos Contains a Sacred Sanctuary

One of the notable features of Kommos is a sacred sanctuary dedicated to a Minoan deity, possibly the goddess Rhea. The sanctuary area includes an altar and various religious artifacts, indicating the religious significance of the site.

It Offers Spectacular Views

Located on a hill overlooking the Libyan Sea, Kommos provides visitors with breathtaking views of the pristine coastline and the surrounding landscapes. The natural beauty combined with the historical significance makes it a must-visit destination.

FAQs

1. What is the history of Kommos?

Kommos has a rich history dating back to the Minoan period and was an important trading center in the Mediterranean. The site has evidence of occupation from the Middle Minoan period (circa 1900 BCE) to the Hellenistic period (circa 146 BCE).

2. How can I visit Kommos?

Kommos is accessible by road and is located on the southern coast of Crete. There is a parking area near the site, and guided tours are available for visitors who want a more in-depth experience.

3. What are some notable features of Kommos?

Kommos is known for its impressive architectural structures, including a large Minoan palace complex and a sanctuary dedicated to the god Dionysus. The site also boasts beautifully preserved mosaics, which provide a glimpse into the skill and artistry of the ancient inhabitants.

4. Are there any ongoing excavations at Kommos?

Yes, there are still ongoing excavations at Kommos, led by archaeologists who are continuously uncovering new discoveries and shedding light on the history of the site. Visitors may have the opportunity to witness these excavations in progress during their visit.

5. Can I bring children to Kommos?

Absolutely! Kommos is a family-friendly site, and children will be fascinated by the ancient ruins and the stories of the past. However, it’s important to ensure their safety and respect for the archaeological site.
