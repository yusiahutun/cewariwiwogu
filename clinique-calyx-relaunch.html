July 2024 · 1 minute read

Media Platforms Design Team

Calyx, which originally launched in 1986 under the beauty brand Prescriptives, transformed the fragrance industry. There was nothing else out there like it. And ever since, its unique citrus scent entangled with a full bouqet of florals as well as fresh green notes have kept many women (and men for that matter) captivated. However, when Prescriptives became strictly available online only, access to the beloved scent was limited. This month, Clinique (an Estee Lauder sister brand to Prescriptives) is redebuting the fragrance—on their site and at their counters—with the same exact original notes, just with their logo now stamped on the bottle.

And if you happen to not be familiar with the scent, simply knowing the true definition of the word 'calyx' gives a hint to its undeniable allure: "the sepals of a flower, typically forming a whorl that encloses the petals and forms a protective layer around a flower in bud."

Clinique Calyx, $52 for 1.7 oz, clinique.com.

Nicole Catanese is a beauty and wellness editor.
