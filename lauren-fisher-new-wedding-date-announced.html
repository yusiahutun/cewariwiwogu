Ochai Agbaji is a basketball player for the Utah Jazz of the National Basketball Association (NBA). 

He was awarded a unanimous first-team All-American and Big 12 Player of the Year as a senior at the University of Kansas in 2022. He was also voted the Final Four Most Outstanding Player (MOP) after leading the Jayhawks to a national title.

The Cleveland Cavaliers selected Agbaji with the 14th overall choice in the 2022 NBA draft. However, before playing a regular-season game in Cleveland, he was dealt to the Utah Jazz in September 2022.

He has already signed a contract with the Utah Jazz, and it is reported that his salary, according to the contract, is worth millions. Here is all we know about it, as well as his net worth and family!

Ochai Agbaji’s salary and net worth

Agbaji is one of the fastest-rising stars in the NBA, and his career has just started. But despite being a newcomer, it is reported that his net worth is approximately $6 million.

That said, his net worth is bound to fluctuate because of his salary and the contract he has signed with the Utah Jazz. In the 2022/2023 season, his salary was $3,918,360. According to his contract, his salary will reportedly increase significantly with every season.

Ochai Agbaji has a high net worth due to his salary, according to his NBA contract. (Source: Instagram)

His salary for the 2023/2024 season is $4,114,200; for the 2024/25 season, it is $4,310,280; for the 2025/26 season, he will be earning $6,383,525; and for the 2026/27 season, he will be getting a whopping $8,879,483. 

Moreover, his contract includes around $100,000 in likely incentives. He is also bound to get bonuses and endorsements in the future as he advances in his career.

As a result, Agbaji’s net worth will be significantly higher in the future than it is right now because of his salary and contract with the Utah Jazz.

Ochai Agbaji’s family and career

Agbaji was raised in Kansas City, Missouri, with his family but was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. Initially, he chose football as his career.

A picture of Ochai Agbaji with his parents (Photo: Twitter)

He grew up playing football, both club football and Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) basketball, thanks to his father’s support. He quit football as a sophomore at Oak Park High School in Kansas City, Missouri, to concentrate on his basketball career.

During his Oak Park basketball career, he did not earn any Power Five college offers until his final year. 

He was selected Kansas City Star All-Metro Player of the Year as a senior after averaging 27.6 points and 8.6 rebounds per game for Oak Park. According to 247Sports and Rivals, the athlete was a three-star recruit.

On February 8, 2018, he chose Kansas over Texas A&M and Wisconsin to play collegiate basketball. Agbaji was a part of the scout team during his freshman year at the University of Kansas.

His redshirt was removed in early January 2019 because Kansas needed depth after Udoka Azubuike sustained a season-ending injury and Silvio De Sousa faced eligibility difficulties. He earned his first career start on January 29, 2019, scoring 24 points, grabbing seven rebounds, and swiping the ball twice in a defeat against Texas. 

In his following game, a victory over Texas Tech, he got his first double-double. For his two efforts, Agbaji was named Big 12 Conference Newcomer of the Week. As a freshman, he averaged 8.5 points and 4.6 rebounds per game in 22 games, including 16 starts. 

He made his sophomore season debut on November 5, 2019, scoring 15 points in a defeat against fourth-ranked Duke at the Champions Classic. He earned Honourable Mention All-Big 12 during his second season.

He averaged 10 points and 4.2 rebounds per game as a sophomore and was second on the team with 46 three-pointers. As a junior, he scored 14.1 points per game while shooting 42% from the field. 

He declared for the NBA draft on April 8, 2021, while still retaining his collegiate eligibility. On November 9, 2021, Agbaji made his senior season debut, scoring 29 points in an 87-74 victory over Michigan State at the Champions Classic. 

He scored a career-high 37 points in a 94-91 double overtime win over Texas Tech on January 24, 2022. At the end of the season, Agbaji was selected Big 12 Player of the Year and a unanimous first-team All-American. 

Kansas won the 2022 NCAA tournament, and he was voted tournament MVP after scoring 12 points in the final game. He declared for the NBA draft on April 24, 2022. The Cleveland Cavaliers picked him with the 14th overall choice in the 2022 NBA draft, and the athlete signed his rookie scale deal with the Cavaliers on July 2, 2022.

Agbaji was dealt to the Utah Jazz in exchange for Donovan Mitchell on September 1, 2022, along with Collin Sexton, Lauri Markkanen, three first-round selections, and two pick swaps. On October 19, Agbaji made his NBA debut, playing one minute in a 123-102 victory over the Denver Nuggets.

Agbaji set a career-best with 28 points against the Nuggets on April 8, 2023. Currently, he is still pursuing his career with the Utah Jazz.

In fact, he is not the only one from his family involved in sports. His father, Olofu, who immigrated to the United States from Nigeria when he was 17 years old, was an avid football fan.

Olofu, along with Agbaji’s mother, Erica, were basketball players at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee. Similarly, Agbaji’s sister, Orie, played volleyball in Texas. All in all, Agbaji comes from a family with a major interest in sports.
