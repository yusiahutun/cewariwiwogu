Scott Thomas reveals he lost 20K gambling as a student and would search nan's bags for cash

October 2024 · 5 minute read

Scott Thomas has revealed that he blew £20,000 gambling in casinos when he was a student and became so 'desperate' for cash he'd search through his nan's old bags.

The former Love Island star, 32, candidly chatted about his former problem on the latest episode of podcast Mancs On The Mic alongside brothers Ryan and Adam.

Reflecting on his gambling days, Scott said: 'When I was a student, I had a really bad gambling problem. It really got hold of me, and no one knew how bad it was.

Candid: Scott Thomas has revealed that he blew £20,000 gambling in casinos when he was a student while speaking on his podcast with brothers Adam and Ryan

'I was going out after student nights and ending up in a casino, losing thousands of pounds of my student loan and everything else.

'The next day I would wake up at eight in the morning and waiting outside the bookies on my own to try to win it back.

'It was so bad. I couldn't tell anyone how bad it was at first. You guys [his actor brothers] just thought it was after nights out.'

Scott continued: 'It feels so weird, like such a different chapter in my life, I feel like it wasn't me but that's what happens, it wraps you up.'

Family: The Love Island star, 32, also told his two brothers that he got so 'desperate' he would search through his nan's old bags for any stray £20 notes (pictured recording their podcast)

The reality star then revealed a secret to his brothers, admitting that at his lowest point he would search through his grandmother's bag for money.

He said: 'And I remember, this is a secret. I've never told anyone this, but I remember being around at me nan's, and I remember being that desperate that I was checking her old handbags for £20s and stuff like that.

'It was so bad. I was about 18, maybe a bit older at 20, or something like that.'

Ryan and Adam were audibly shocked at the revelation, with them both admitting that they remembered when Scott was constantly asking them for money. 

Former Coronation Street actor Ryan then joined in the conversation saying that the brothers were each given £20k inheritance from their nana when she died.

Getting help: Scott (pictured) eventually went to his older brother Ryan, 36, who used to be on Coronation Street, for help and was taken to Gamblers Anonymous

He said: 'I remember seeing you going round and giving out money [from the inheritance] to people you borrowed off. 

'That night you came to me and broke down in tears and said "Ryan, I've got a problem and I need help". 

'And I'm so glad I didn't reach out to you because I think we're the sort of people who have to make those decisions for ourselves, no-one can tell us what to do, you have to be ready when you're ready.'

Ryan continued: 'We took you to Gambler's Anonymous, I couldn't come in I had to sit with the other family and friends downstairs.'  

The Scott brothers are very close and have recently been supporting each other through the loss of their dad, Seventies music legend Dougie James who was 72.

Tough few months: It comes after the Thomas brothers lost their father Dougie James, who was a Seventies music legend, at the age of 72 (Scott and his dad are pictured together)

In December Scott vowed not to 'spiral out of control' following his father's death.  

Taking to Instagram, the Love Island star, gave a brave speech to his 917k followers, as he thanked fans for their support, detailed the time he lived with his dad shortly before his death and gushed about being lucky to have his father.  

Scott took to Instagram to pay tribute to his father and also speak to fans about how he was coping with his grief and his determination to stay strong. 

He said: 'I wanted to first of all thank everyone for the lovely messages for me and the boys, it means a lot. I'm OK, I know a lot of people are worried about me... It's just a very sad time and anyone who loses their dad knows how sad it is...

'But at the same time, I'm OK, I'm strong and I'm going to use this pain to drive me forward and channel it in the right way.'

Grieving: Scott later vowed not to 'spiral out of control' following his father death

Scott then made his determined vow, saying: 'I'm not going to spiral out of control. I've been back trying to train... at the same time I'm going to take some time for myself as well because I know how important it is to get that space.'

He revealed that his father had lived with him prior to his passing, saying: 'I'm just really grateful for the time I got with my dad...  

'I had the most amazing six months with him living under my roof. We had the best time ever and I'm just so grateful and I just can't believe how lucky I am to have had him there with me and I cherished every moment...

'Make sure you take that time for your family, make sure you keep them close, that's my biggest lesson from this.' 

In 2019 Adam, Scott and former Coronation Street star Ryan headed to India with their father for an ITV documentary, Mancs In Mumbai, where they explored their heritage for a six-part series.
