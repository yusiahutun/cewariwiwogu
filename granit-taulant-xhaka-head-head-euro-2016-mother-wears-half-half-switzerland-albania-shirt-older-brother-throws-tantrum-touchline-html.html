Granit vs Taulant Xhaka at Euro 2016: Mother wears Switzerland-Albania shirt

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Elmaze Xhaka, the mother of Granit and Taulant, showed her support for both Albania and Switzerland by wearing a half-and-half shirt in stands at the Stade Bollaert-Delelis in Lens.

The Xhaka brothers went up against each other at Euro 2016 ‒ Granit for Switzerland and Tauland for Albania ‒ as they got their tournament's under way.

Arsenal's new £35million signing, Granit, represents Switzerland after being born and growing up there, while his older brother bears the colours of his family's Albanian heritage.

Albania's Taulant Xhaka (right) challenges his brother, Switzerland's Granit Xhaka, during the Euro 2016 tie

Elmaze was sat next to Granit's girlfriend Leonita Lekaj for the game and their father, Ragip, will also have had mixed emotions at seeing his sons face off in France.

Ragip spent three years as a political prisoner in a prison cell in Kosovo and it was only after his release at the age of 25 that he was allowed to begin his life properly in Basle.

He raised two professional footballers and Taulant, 25, and Granit, 23, did not hold back here.

They hugged before kick-off, having this week acknowledged in interviews that they are not just brothers but best friends, and were friendly until the game got going as they battled throughout.

Taulant was substituted in the 62nd minute after recently returning from a thigh injury but threw a tantrum on the touchline, angrily throwing a water bottle as he was taken off.

Albania's Xhaka was not happy at being substituted after 61 minutes and threw a water bottle

Albania's Xhaka threw a water bottle on the touchline as he was taken off during the Euro 2016 tie

Xhaka was taken off after 61 minutes but was not happy and threw a tantrum on the touchline

Neither of the Xhaka brothers are angels.

Granit was sent off three times for Borussia Monchengladbach in the Bundesliga last season, though that did not dissuade Arsene Wenger from signing him.

Taulant has been suspended twice at Basle for slapping and pulling the hair of opponents, and was involved in a near-riot when Albania played Serbia in Belgrade during Euro 2016 qualification.

The Euro 2016 tie ended 1-0 to Switzerland, despite Albania putting up a fight after having Lorik Cana, their captain, sent off for a second bookable offence in the 37th minute.
