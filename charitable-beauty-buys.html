Charitable beauty products - Beauty brands donating to charity

August 2024 · 6 minute read

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Louis Vuitton Perfumed I (RED) Candle

Louis Vuitton Perfumed I (RED) Candle

Credit: Louis Vuitton

AIDS is one of the most stigmatized of diseases but Louis Vuitton is working to fight both the illness and its misconceptions using the funds raised by this limited edition red candle. For every candle (with its blend of raspberry, peony and hints of spice) that's sold, $60 will be donated to (RED).  

Jo Malone Lily Of The Valley & Ivy Charity Candle

Jo Malone Lily Of The Valley & Ivy Charity Candle

On the 10th October, Jo Malone London will donate 100 per cent of the retail price (minus VAT) of its new limited-edition Lily of the Valley candle to projects that support individuals and their families facing mental health difficulties. So, lighting it isn't just an act of self-care for yourself, its a chance to help care for others, too. 

ghd Platinum+ Ink On Pink Collection

ghd Platinum+ Ink On Pink Collection

Ghd has been supporting breast cancer charities for an incredible 15 years, but the brand's latest Ink On Pink collection is perhaps one of the most powerful (and prettiest) yet. This year, the brand shines a spotlight on the incredible work of tattoo artist David Allen and his acclaimed work with mastectomy scars that help women build back their self-esteem after breast cancer. The limited-edition styler features an original floral design by Allen, with £10 from every sale being donated to Breast Cancer Now.

Spacemasks Rose

Spacemasks Rose

£15 for five 

Next time you need to have some relaxing me-time, why not choose a Spacemask? Not only are the self-warming masks are shortcut to ultimate calm, but a donation of £1 from every box of the new rose-scented variation will be donated to the Royal Marsden Cancer Charity, contributing to the £70 million appeal to build a new state-of-the-art facility at the centre. 

Kiehl's Kiehl’s Made Better x John Legend Rare Earth Deep Pore Cleansing Masque Mask

Kiehl's Kiehl’s Made Better x John Legend Rare Earth Deep Pore Cleansing Masque Mask

Kiehl's has a long-standing tradition of supporting charities that campaign for sustainability through its Kiehl's Made Better initiative alongside the singer John Legend. This year sees the return of the brand's best-selling deep cleansing face mask, with £5 being donated to the Hubbub, a charity that creates environmental campaigns. 

Charlotte Tilbury Hot Lips 2 Lipsticks

Charlotte Tilbury Hot Lips 2 Lipsticks

If you needed another excuse to treat yourself to a Charlotte Tilbury lipstick, the make-up artist has revealed not only a new Hot Lips 2 collection but also a £1 million pledge to support the work Women for Women International does for survivors of war. The decision comes after Tilbury discovered "the story of a woman who felt empowered by her red lipstick while living in conflict" and will help more women complete year-long training programme to improve their lives.

Neal's Yard Remedies Bee Lovely range

Neal's Yard Remedies Bee Lovely range

From £4

Through sales of its Bee Lovely collection, Neal's Yard Remedies donates money to help save Britain's bees. With experts warning that honey bees could disappear entirely from the UK in just a few years, these insects need protecting if we want to continue to have access to organic honey's skincare benefits.
