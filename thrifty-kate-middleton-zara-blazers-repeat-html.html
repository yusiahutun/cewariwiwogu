How thrifty Kate Middleton makes FULL use of her Zara habit - and wears their adaptable blazers on r

July 2024 · 9 minute read

If it isn't broken, don't fix it, is the Princess of Wales’ motto for her casual-cool outings.

And nothing is more important in her work-wear wardrobe than the Zara blazer.

Thanks to its boxy shape, lapel collar and structured shoulders, it is the perfect choice for 'transitional dressing' - taking the wearer through from one season to the next.

Where in the past the coat-dress reigned supreme, recently it has been Zara tailoring that she has worn on repeat with styles recycled season after season.

Kate Middleton may prefer custom-made gowns on the red carpet, but high street fashion usually does the trick while she’s out performing royal duties, . 

Kate, the Princess of Wales, is often spotted re-wearing pieces from high-street favourite Zara

Making great efforts to be seen as relatable in her wardrobe and re-wearing affordable brands, she often combines pieces from high-street favourite Zara with designer separates and accessories. 

It’s this winning mix of high and low that has made the princess a style star.

Last Sunday, she pulled it off once again when watching the rugby in a white Zara blazer with wide-legged Roland Mouret trousers and a classic Chanel bag. 

It's a tried and tested fashion formula. 

Not only was this the third time we have seen the tailored boucle piece, but she has worn the style in many other colour iterations too, all the way back to early royal appearances making it a wardrobe must have.

A couple of months later it was worn in March to speak with Iceland Food's Richard Walker for the Shaping Us campaign, paired with Accessorize earrings.

Wearing a textured red double breasted blazer from Zara, she kept the look casual and chic with black LK Bennett tailored trousers and Boden pumps.

The blazer was also previously seen on a trip to the University of Copenhagen in February 2022 where it was dressed up with a ME+EM top, Roland Mouret trousers and an Aspinal of London bag and again during a football match in June 2021.

The following year in June the princess picked the same blazer but this time accentuated the single gold button and gave it a nautical edge with buttoned J Crew trousers and the same J Crew shoes as before. 

In November 2020 the blazer came back out the wardrobe for an Early Years video message and again in July 2021 at Wembley Stadium to attend the EURO 2020 final between England and Italy.

The Princess of Wales showed the versatility of this cobalt blue double breasted blazer when she wore it across three different events, each time giving it a new lease of life. 

First seen in March 2021 for an interview to mark International Day of the Midwife, she accessorised it with Orelia London earrings for a business chic ensemble. 

A couple of months later in May it was seen again during a Scotland tour and this time it was buttoned up and layered over a Hope pleated midi skirt and paired with brown suede Gianvito Rossi pumps, a Métier bag and an Amaia face mask. 

Most recently, the princess recycled the bold blazer in October 2023 when attending a mental health fitness workshop run by SportsAid. Here, it was dressed down with Mother Denim jeans, a Ralph Lauren top and Veja sneakers for a relaxed and casual sporting look.

To celebrate St Patrick’s Day in 2021 Kate and William joined other world leaders in sharing a special video message and they dressed for the occasion with the princess choosing an emerald green double breasted blazer which became an instant sell-out. 

Keeping with the patriotic theme and giving a nod to Irish tradition, she accessorised the look with Daniella Draper’s gold shamrock necklace and matching mini hoop earrings which were first debuted during their royal trip to Ireland the previous year.

It was tonal dressing for the Princess of Wales when she was back to work in June 2022, days after a whirlwind weekend of Platinum Jubilee celebrations. 

Visiting a baby bank, the stylish royal wore a cream-hued buttonless blazer worn over a co-ordinating top and navy Roland Mouret trousers. She wore her hair down and completed her elegant look with matching Gianvito Rossi pumps and a Massimo Dutti clutch.

A mirror image of the outfit was recycled in October 2022 during a special news segment for World Mental Health Day recorded for the BBC, although this time a Laura Lombardi chunky necklace was added.
