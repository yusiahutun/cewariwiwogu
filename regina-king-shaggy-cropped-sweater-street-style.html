Regina King Channels the '90s in Shaggy Cropped Sweater and Jeans

February 2024 · 2 minute read

While interviewing Regina King on Thursday night, talk show host Jimmy Kimmel pulled out a newspaper clipping from 1988, in which the future Oscar-winning actor said she wanted to visit Europe because she was “into clothes.” It’s no surprise King’s love of fashion dates back to her teenage years, because her sartorial versatility—from the red carpet to the rehearsal studio—is seriously impressive.

On her way to the talk show, the Shirley star was spotted in a shaggy gray crop-top sweater and light-wash jeans—a fit that screamed ’90s street style. Her crop top had an abstract white pattern snaking across it, pieces of the fabric sticking off the sweater like fringe. The high-neck outerwear included a V-shaped collar with silver arrow-shaped studs on one side, which could be looped through knots on the other. Down low, the actor wore a pair of wide-leg jeans, which she cuffed once so that her black ballet flats were still visible. And thank goodness she did, because we would hate to have missed the golden crystal-shaped accessories at the front of the shoes’ toplines.

RB/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

King rocked a set of long dreadlocks for Kimmel’s show, sported brass hoop earrings, and carried a cream Louis Vuitton bag on her shoulder.

The actor and producer is the cover star of Harper’s Bazaar’s April 2024 Possibility issue, and she is currently promoting her new Netflix film, Shirley, about the first Black congresswoman, Shirley Chisholm. Earlier this week, she attended the movie’s premiere in a flowing asymmetrical mini gown from Schiaparelli.

Aliah Anderson//Getty Images

The LBD included a golden chain neckline, which wrapped around King’s neck in a halter style. One half of the dress looked fairly standard, while the other side was composed of a ruched draping material, which flowed off the dress into a U-shaped strip that was tied around King’s wrist and connected with another gold chain. To match these metal materials, the If Beale Street Could Talk star wore shiny golden heels from Le Silla.
