Juno star Jason Bateman booed after jumping the queue for an iPhone 4

Updated: 10:44 EST, 25 June 2010

Hollywood star Jason Bateman incurred the wrath of hundred of Apple iPhone fans yesterday when he jumped the queue to purchase the latest model.

The Juno actor, 41, had been patiently waiting in line with an estimated 2,000 people outside the Apple store at The Grove shopping centre yesterday when a store employee spotted him.

While the star had been content to wait alongside the mere mortals, he was no doubt thrilled to be offered the opportunity to jump to the front of the queue.

Patient: Jason Bateman was content to wait in line with an estimated 2,000 people outside the Apple store at the Grove shopping centre in Hollywood

As Bateman was escorted into the store, he was booed and jeered by angry shoppers who had been waiting for hours for a chance to buy the new iPhone 4, which went on sale yesterday.

Trying to ignore the boos, Bateman entered the store and picked up the latest technie must-have and was welcomed by more criticism as he hurried embarrassed out of the store.

One queuer told Us Weekly: 'Everyone literally started booing and hissing.'

Unimpressed: Stunned iPhone fans aren't amused to see Bateman being escorted to the front of the queue

Another added: 'The crowd freaked and booed, and he put his head down.'

Another celebrity spotted picking up the new iPhone 4 yesterday was Rihanna's baseball player boyfriend Matt Kemp.

The Bajan singer, 22, patiently waited at Kemp, 25, upgraded his old iPhone to the newer model.

Boys and their toys: Bateman heads into the Apple store to pick up the must-have gadget

Apple's latest smartphone is slimmer and more powerful than its predecessor and features the new OS 4 operating system as standard, which makes multi-tasking third party apps possible for the first time.

The iPhone 4 offers video calling, a higher-resolution display and the ability to record and view high-definition video. It also features an oil-resistant glass screen to stop grubby fingers smearing the display.

Video conversations are only available between owners of iPhone 4 handsets connected via a wi-fi link to the internet. There are plans to make them available over mobile phone networks in the future
