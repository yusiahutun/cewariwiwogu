Karolina Muchova is a career-focused Czech tennis player who holds the 10th position in the WTA Singles ranking at this moment. The year 2023 turned out to be the probably best in her professional journey so far as she gained her highest-ever 10th spot in the ranking in August 2023. She started playing professionally in 2013 and with every year passing, she is improving her game. Talking about her struggle then last year in 2022, she was ranked at 235th spot and bounced back all the way into the top 10 this year. 

Early Life

Born on August 21, 1996, in Olomouc in the eastern Czech Republic, Karolina is the daughter of Josef Mucha who is a former Czech footballer. She played sports with her brother when they were kids. She was introduced to tennis by her father when she was 7 years old.

Karolina once mentioned that Roger Federer was her tennis idol. Karolina Muchova has never talked about her education qualification or has never mentioned the name of her school or college. She chooses to keep her education qualification secret.

Karolina Muchova Career

Karolina had a tennis court near her house where she used to play with her brother. At the age of 12, she decided to choose tennis over handball and that’s when she decided the path of her career. In October 2013, at the age of 17, Muchova started playing in the ITF Women’s Circuit.

She played her first tournament in Dubrovnik, reaching the second round. Her first tournament was worth $10K. She won her first ITF title in July 2014 in Michalovce, Slovakia. Jessica had low performances till 2016 after which she won her second singles ITF title at a $10K event in Sharm El Sheikh. She won another event two weeks later which was held at the same location.

In July 2017, she reached the finals of the $75K event ITS Cup in Olomouc where she lost against her own countrymate. She made her main draw debut in the 2017 Korea Open in the WTA Tour after defeating two low-ranked players and then lost in the first round against Priscilla Hon.

She made her main draw debut at a Grand Slam event at the 2018 US Open winning three qualifying matches. After winning the opening matches, she won against the two-time major champion in the second round to score her first top-20 victory and made it to the third round where she lost. In 2019, her performance was enough to take her to the top 100 of the WTA rankings for the first time.

Widespread Fame

She finished 2019 year as the world No. 21, which is her career’s highest singles ranking. In 2020, she gave a weak performance but still, she managed to show minor progress. She reached the second round of the Australian Open for the first time in her career.

She even played at the Qatar Opens for the first time where she recorded her first win over Magda Linette, but then lost in the second round. After a six-month suspension due to the pandemic, she played at the Cincinnati Open where she lost the second. She spent the whole year on the top 30 list.
