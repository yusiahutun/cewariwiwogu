Jadian Sinead Zeiders, 26, is accused of helping to beat William Dehman Jr., 54, to death along with two other men

A Florida woman has been charged with murder as police say she helped to beat a man to death after claiming he masturbated at her.

Jadian Sinead Zeiders, 26, is facing a charge of second-degree murder over the death of 53-year-old social worker William Denham Jr. back in January.

Zeiders allegedly told police that Denham, who was openly gay, broke into her back yard in Tampa on January 14 and masturbated at her through the window.

Officers say she claimed to have hidden in her laundry room while Denham was in the yard, before two men living at the same address arrived home.

Zeiders then pointed Denham out to the two men, who began following him down the street, according to a police report seen by the Tampa Bay Times.

An argument followed and Zeiders said Denham eventually lashed out, causing the men to attack in self-defense, the report says.

The woman then claims a black man she didn't know emerged from the bushes, kicked Denham in the head, and started going through his pockets.

Zeiders claims she watched the ambulance arrive, at which point the black man walked away and refused medical treatment.

Zeiders accused Denham of breaking into her yard and masturbating at her, saying she pointed him out to two male companions, who then beat him. She denies involvement. Denhams body was found at this intersection in Tampa in January 

But multiple witnesses told officers they saw a woman and two men attack Denham as he was walking along the street.

An anonymous tipster also named Zeiders and the two men, who have not yet been identified publicly because they have not been arrested or charged. 

Dan Kane, Dehman's life-long partner, believes the trio beat his companion to death after attempting to rob him and discovering he only had a few dollars in his pocket.

Kane rubbished the masturbation story, saying it would have been completely out of character for Denham, especially considering his sexual orientation.

Zeiders has previous convictions for heroin possession and failure to appear in court on a driving infraction charge.

Police say they have identified two suspects in addition to Zeiders, but are still sifting through 'conflicting information'.
