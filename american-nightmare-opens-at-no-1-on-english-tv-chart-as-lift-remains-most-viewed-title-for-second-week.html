American Nightmare Opens at No. 1 on English TV Chart as Lift Remains Most-Viewed Title

April 2024 · 2 minute read

“American Nightmare” opened at No. 1 on the Netflix’s English TV chart following its Jan. 17 premiere. The documentary racked up 21.4 million views during the Jan. 15-21 viewing window as viewers followed the “real-life Gone Girl” story. Thus, taking down Harlan Coben’s mystery-thriller “Fool Me Once” for the top spot after its two-week run.

Now in the No. 2 spot, the series earned 11.2 million views and putting its total views to date at 72.2 million, so it’s no surprise that Netflix ordered two more shows from the author. “Missing You” and “Run Away” will be the next adaptations to come to Netflix. Both produced by Quay Street Productions, Coben will serve as executive producer through his company, Final Twist Productions.

More from Variety

“Boy Swallows Universe” came in at No.3 this week as the limited series picked up 4 million total views in its second week among the list of titles. That’s a slight increase since last week’s recorded 3.6, which landed the show at No. 5 on the chart in its debut.

Seasons 1 and 2 of “Yellowstone” also joined the Top 10 this week at No. 4 (3.7 million) and No. 9 (1.7 million), respectively, after the show debuted on the streamer on Jan. 15 keeping Paramount in the conversation following the departure of “Young Sheldon,” which marked its last entry on the Top 10 during the Dec. 25-31 interval.

“The Brothers Sun” dropped to No. 5 on the chart with 2.7 million views. It lands just above “My Life With the Walter Boys” which picked up another 2.1 million views.

Newcomers to the chart, “The Stranger” and Season 1 of “LouderMilk” recorded 2 million views and 1.7 million views, respectively.

On the film side, “Lift” continues its solid performance, landing another week as the most-viewed title with 36.7 million views — that’s a slight jump up from its debut which recorded 32.8 million views. Starring Kevin Hart, director F. Gary Gray’s action-thriller reached the Top 10 in 93 countries.

Elsewhere on the chart is Season 3 of “Sonic Prime” (1.8 million views).
